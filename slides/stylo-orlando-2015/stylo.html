Background

Years ago, Brendan peered future and made the following observations:
  • More cores, fewer clock cycles
  • Parallel software will win
  • Gecko is mostly serial
  • Making it parallel would be really hard
Especially on mobile.
Long term.
(2x) Same goes for its competitors.

Proposed the following:

Let's Rewrite C++ From Scratch, and Use the Result to Rewrite Gecko From Scratch!

Oldest truism: boil-ocean basically impossible.
Other truism: Can do impossible with great team.
Hired great team - turned out really great.

August, plan:

Boil the Ocean... Incrementally!

Take the parts of Servo that offer the most powerful, unique wins over other engines,
and try to plug them into Gecko. Get it hooked up, validate performance wins, then dump
Platform engineers on it to make it feature-complete / web-compatible.

The Syle System.

Natural place to start.
  • Fast, Parallel.
  • Large part of layout overhead (~50%).
  • Relatively Mature.
  • Relatively Self-contained.
  • Separates the Parallelizable and Non-Parallelizable Parts of the Engine.
If we extend to layout, only need to cut in one place.
